# Developer Manifesto

## Principles to Build By

In the realm of code and creation, we stand united by these fundamental truths that guide our journey through the <u>developer lifecycle</u>—from eager beginner to seasoned architect.

---

### Code Quality

The foundation of our craft lies in writing code that serves not just the machine, but the human mind.

- **<u>Readability over Performance</u>**: Code is read far more often than it's written. Clarity is the ultimate currency in our profession.
- **<u>Simplicity is Sophistication</u>**: The best code is not clever—it's understandable. Complex solutions often hide simple problems.
- **<u>Documentation is Code</u>**: Comments and documentation are not optional; they are essential bridges between minds across time.

### Open Source Spirit

Our community thrives on the principle that knowledge shared is knowledge multiplied. The <u>collaborative mindset</u> defines the evolution from individual contributor to community leader.

- **<u>Share to Grow</u>**: Knowledge hoarded is knowledge wasted. Open source builds communities that transcend individual capabilities.
- **<u>Give Credit</u>**: Attribution is not just courtesy—it's respect for the craft and recognition of collective intelligence.
- **<u>Collaborate</u>**: The best solutions emerge from diverse perspectives working together. No developer is an island.

---

### Technical Excellence

The mark of a mature developer lies not just in what they build, but how they ensure it stands the test of time.

- **<u>Test Everything</u>**: Code without tests is code that cannot be trusted. Testing is the bridge between confidence and chaos.
- **<u>Security First</u>**: Every line of code is a potential security vulnerability. Security is not a feature—it's a fundamental requirement.
- **<u>Performance Matters</u>**: But never at the cost of maintainability or user experience. Optimization without understanding is premature.

### Continuous Learning

The <u>learning journey</u> never ends in our field. From junior to senior, from senior to architect, each phase demands new skills and perspectives.

- **<u>Embrace Change</u>**: Technology evolves rapidly; adaptability is survival. The developer who stops learning stops growing.
- **<u>Learn from Failures</u>**: Every bug is a lesson; every crash is an opportunity. Failure is the most honest teacher we have.
- **<u>Mentor Others</u>**: Teaching reinforces understanding and builds stronger teams. The cycle of knowledge perpetuates our craft.

---

### User-Centric Development

At the heart of every technical decision lies a human being. The <u>user experience</u> is the ultimate measure of our success.

- **<u>Users First</u>**: Every technical decision should serve the end user. Technology without purpose is just expensive noise.
- **<u>Accessibility is Essential</u>**: Technology should empower everyone, not just the able. Inclusive design is not optional—it's fundamental.
- **<u>Privacy by Design</u>**: User data is sacred; protect it like your own. Trust, once lost, is nearly impossible to regain.

### Inclusive Community Standards

Building software is a <u>team sport</u>. Our strength lies in our diversity and our commitment to creating spaces where everyone can thrive.

- **<u>Zero Tolerance for Harassment</u>**: We maintain a harassment-free environment for all participants. Respect is non-negotiable.
- **<u>Respectful Communication</u>**: All forms of communication should uplift, not demean others. Words have power—use them wisely.
- **<u>Inclusive Participation</u>**: Everyone deserves to be here and enjoy their experience without fear. Belonging is a right, not a privilege.
- **<u>Speak Up</u>**: We encourage polite engagement when disrespectful behavior is observed. Silence enables harm.

### Resistance to Toxic Industry Practices

The <u>developer lifecycle</u> should be marked by growth, not burnout. We reject the toxic practices that plague our industry.

- **<u>No to Crunch Culture</u>**: "Sleep is for the weak" is a lie told by managers who can't plan properly. Sustainable pace beats unsustainable speed.
- **<u>No to Imposter Syndrome Exploitation</u>**: You're not "lucky to be here"—you earned your place through skill and dedication. Confidence is not arrogance.
- **<u>No to Burnout Glorification</u>**: Being "always available" isn't dedication; it's poor boundary management. Rest is not laziness.
- **<u>No to Knowledge Hoarding</u>**: "Job security through obscurity" is the philosophy of the insecure and mediocre. Sharing knowledge builds stronger teams.
- **<u>No to Perfection Paralysis</u>**: "Ship it when it's perfect" is the anthem of products that never see daylight. Done is better than perfect.

---

### Developer Immunity Declaration

We are <u>immune to manipulation</u> and <u>protected by our principles</u>. The journey from junior to senior is marked by these defenses:

**We are immune to:**
- Pressure to work 80-hour weeks for "passion"
- Guilt trips about "not being dedicated enough"
- Fear-mongering about job security
- Manipulation through false urgency
- Gaslighting about our capabilities

**Our shields are:**
- <u>Solid technical skills</u> that speak louder than words
- <u>Strong professional boundaries</u> that protect our well-being
- <u>Supportive developer communities</u> that lift us up
- <u>Continuous learning mindset</u> that keeps us relevant
- <u>Unwavering self-respect</u> that cannot be shaken

## Anti-Harassment Policy

We are dedicated to providing a harassment-free and inclusive experience for everyone regardless of gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disabilities, neurodiversity, physical appearance, body size, ethnicity, nationality, race, age, religion, or other protected category.

### Our Standards

All participants must abide by the following principles:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat everyone with respect and acknowledge that everyone deserves to be here
- **Speak Up**: Politely engage when you or others are disrespected—the person may not be aware of their behavior
- **Consider Impact**: Think about what you're saying and how it would feel if said to you

### Zero Tolerance Policy

We have a ZERO TOLERANCE POLICY for harassment of any kind, including but not limited to:

- Stalking or following
- Deliberate intimidation
- Harassing photography or recording
- Sustained disruption of events
- Offensive verbal language
- Language that reinforces social structures of domination
- Sexual imagery and language in public spaces
- Inappropriate physical contact
- Unwelcome sexual or physical attention
- Physical or cyber threats

This applies to all aspects of our community: talks, forums, workshops, social media, and all participants including attendees, partners, sponsors, volunteers, and staff.

### Enforcement

Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately. We will review every allegation and respond appropriately. Violators may face consequences including warnings, expulsion from events, or permanent bans from our community.

## The Commitment

We commit to these principles not as rigid rules, but as <u>guiding stars</u>. In the vast universe of software development, they provide direction when the path is unclear.

The <u>developer lifecycle</u> is a journey of continuous growth—from writing your first "Hello World" to architecting systems that serve millions. At every stage, these principles light the way.

**Build with intention.**  
**Code with conscience.**  
**Ship with pride.**

---

*This manifesto is a living document. It grows with our community and evolves with our understanding of what it means to be a developer in the modern world. The <u>developer lifecycle</u> continues, and so does our commitment to excellence.*
